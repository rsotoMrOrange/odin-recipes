Chilaquiles Rojos

Chilaquiles are a mexican typical dish, especially eaten when trying to recover from a hangover or 'cruda' as we call it here in Mexico. Chilaquiles are basically fried tortillas bathed in a green or red sauce. Don't let the color deceive you, normally the green sauce is the spicy one. You could also add a protein, such as chicken, meat or eggs. Lastly, add cheese, cream and onion on top and you're good to go.

Ingredients (4 people)

  • For the sauce:
    • 3 tomatoes
    • 6 clean guajillo chili, no veins nor seeds
    • 2 garlic cloves
    • 1/4 of onion
    • 1 teaspoon of olive oil
    • 2 branches of epazote (stinky sweat)
    • salt
  • For the chilaquiles:
    • 4 eggs
    • 150ml of olive oil
    • 400g of chips
    • 200ml of cream
    • 250g of fresh cheese
    • 1/2 onion in cubes
    • 2 cups of beans

Steps

  • For the sauce:
    1. Boil the tomatoes in water for 3 minutes
    2. Add the guajillo chilis and boil for 2 more minutes
    3. Let the pot cool down
    4. Blend the tomatoes and the chilis using the same water from the pot, add the 2 garlic cloves and the onion
    5. Pour the sauce and put it to the side. Add olive oil to a pan; when it's hot, add the sauce, the epazote branches and the salt
    6. Let it boil for 5 minutes and then put it away.
  • For the chilaquiles:
    1. Fry the 4 eggs
    2. Distribute the tortilla chips on the plates y bathe them with the sauce
    3. Add cream, fresh cheese crumbles and onion. Add a fried egg to each egg and beans if you want to
    4. And that's it!
